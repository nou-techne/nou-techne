## Slide 2: The Problem

### Hierarchies centralize power
One organization controls everyone. Autonomy is lost.

### Silos fragment knowledge
Communities can't see each other. Collaboration requires permission.

### Centralized data is vulnerable
One company holds your data. Outages affect everyone.

### Value concentrates
Economic power doesn't flow to the people.

**Can we coordinate without centralizing?**

## Slide 9: Governance by Design

### Local (Hub)
Full autonomy. Who are members. Patronage formula. Which ventures to support.

### Bridge (Two Hubs)
Bilateral consent. What coordination is needed. How to split costs and revenue.

### Guild (Global Craft)
Consensus-seeking. Best practices and standards. Specialization definitions.

### Forest (Ecosystem)
One hub, one vote. Any hub can veto. Affects shared infrastructure.

**Conflicts are expensive. Exit is easy. Incentive: cooperate.**

---

## Slide 10: Why This Matters

### Locally Rooted
Each hub thrives in its specific place. Ecology matters. Community matters.

### Globally Coordinated
Hubs see each other. Knowledge flows. Problems are shared. Leverage increases.

### Economically Just
Value flows based on contribution, not concentration. Patronage over capital.

### Resilient
No single point of failure. Exit is always an option. The system survives disagreement.
