## 4. Basic Governance Rights

### Design Question
*How do you divide voting rights? How often does membership meet? What is quorum?*

### RegenHub Answer

**Voting Structure:**
- One member, one vote — Class 1 only
- Classes 2, 3, and 4 are explicitly non-voting
- This is a deliberate design choice to preserve democratic member control while enabling diverse participation

**Meetings:**
- Annual member meeting required (in-person at 1515 Walnut with virtual participation option)
- Board meetings at regular intervals (recommend monthly during formation)
- Special meetings can be called (see Section 5)

**Quorum:**
- Recommended: 50% of Class 1 members for member meetings (appropriate for small cooperative of 8 organizers)
- As membership grows, quorum percentage should decrease (recommend review at 20+ members)
- Board quorum: simple majority of directors

### Open Questions

- [ ] Can members vote by proxy or absentee ballot?
- [ ] Is electronic/asynchronous voting permitted for routine decisions?
- [ ] What notice period is required for member meetings?
- [ ] How will meeting minutes be recorded and shared?

## 5. How Does the Membership Hold the Board Accountable?

### Design Question
*What additional governance powers do members have? Can members force a special meeting?*

### RegenHub Answer

**Member Powers Beyond Board Elections:**

| Power | Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Amend bylaws | Supermajority of Class 1 + board recommendation | Both required — bylaws are the constitutional foundation |
| Force special meeting | 20% of Class 1 members | Emergency accountability mechanism |
| Approve dissolution/merger | Supermajority of Class 1 | Highest threshold |
| Remove board member | Supermajority of Class 1 | With cause |
| Override board decision | Supermajority of Class 1 | On major strategic issues only |

**Accountability Mechanisms:**
- Annual member satisfaction survey
- Transparent coordination via co-op.us Workshop (all sprint activity, proposals, and decisions are visible to all members)
- Board meeting minutes shared with all Class 1 members
- Financial reports distributed quarterly

**Unique to RegenHub — Transparent Agency:**
The co-op.us Workshop provides real-time visibility into all coordination activity. This is not just a collaboration tool — it is the cooperative's implementation of transparent agency as a governance principle. Every proposal, every sprint, every decision is logged and visible.

## 6. Financial Rights

### Design Question
*What is the patronage formula? For multi-stakeholder co-ops, what is the percentage profit share between classes?*

### RegenHub Answer

**Patronage Formula (proposed, pending FSC):**

| Dimension | Weight | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Labor | 40% | Hours contributed, tracked through coordination infrastructure |
| Revenue | 30% | Revenue generated through ventures and cooperative activities |
| Cash | 20% | Capital contributions |
| Community | 10% | Community participation, education, outreach |

**Key Design Principles:**
- Labor is weighted highest (40%) — reflecting the cooperative principle that people who do the work should receive the largest share
- Revenue contribution rewards ventures that generate cooperative income
- Cash acknowledges capital risk without letting it dominate
- Community participation ensures non-economic contributions are recognized

**Inter-Class Profit Sharing:**
- Only Class 1 members receive patronage dividends
- Class 3 investors receive returns as specified in their investment agreements (rate TBD by FSC)
- Class 2 ventures benefit through infrastructure access rather than direct profit share
- Class 4 members benefit through platform access and cloud credits

**Tax Treatment:**
- LCA elects Subchapter K (partnership) tax treatment
- Allocations must satisfy IRC 704(b) "substantial economic effect" requirements
- Patronage distributions are pre-tax, flowing through to individual members
- Capital accounts maintained per IRC 704(b) regulations

**Founder Considerations:**
- Start.coop asks: do founders receive extra benefit for early risk?
- RegenHub approach: founding organizers build patronage equity through the formula — no separate founder allocation
- Early labor contributions are weighted by the 40% labor dimension

### Open Questions

- [ ] What is the target patronage allocation period? (Annual? Semi-annual?)
- [ ] What percentage of surplus is retained vs. distributed? (Recommend 50% retained for first 3 years)
- [ ] How is labor measured? Hours only, or a points system? (Consider: co-op.us contribution tracking)
- [ ] How is community participation measured?
- [ ] When does patronage distribution begin? (After what revenue threshold or time period?)
- [ ] Is there a cap on individual patronage distributions?

## 7. Board of Directors

### Design Question
*How many people? Who are the first directors? How are they nominated and elected?*

### RegenHub Answer

**Board Size:**
- Recommended initial size: 5 directors (expandable to 7 or 9 by member vote)
- Odd number to prevent ties

**Composition:**
- All board members must be Class 1 members
- Provision for 1-2 outside directors in advisory capacity (non-voting or limited voting)
- Operations steward (currently Todd Youngblood) attends board meetings

**Election Process:**
- Annual election at member meeting
- All Class 1 members eligible to run
- Staggered terms recommended (2-year terms, half elected each year for continuity)
- Nominations open to all Class 1 members

**Board Decision-Making:**

| Decision Type | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Routine business decisions | Simple majority (51%+) |
| Budget approval | Simple majority |
| Membership admission | Simple majority |
| Bylaw change recommendation | Supermajority (66%+) |
| Major financial commitments | Supermajority (66%+) |
| Dissolution recommendation | Supermajority (75%+) |

### Open Questions

- [ ] Who are the initial board members? (All 8 organizers, or a selected subset?)
- [ ] Should the operations steward have a permanent board seat, or rotate?
- [ ] Term limits? (Recommend: 3 consecutive terms maximum, with 1-year cooling period)
- [ ] Are there board committees? (Financial Systems Committee already exists — formalize?)
- [ ] How are board vacancies filled between elections?
- [ ] Is there a formal board orientation process?
