## 1. Cooperative Purpose

**Why does RegenHub exist?**

RegenHub exists to build commons-based infrastructure for the information economy — providing a cooperative alternative to the extractive venture model that concentrates ownership, wealth, and decision-making power in founders and investors.

The cooperative recognizes that the tools an organization uses to track value shape what it can see, and what it can see determines whether it extracts or enriches. RegenHub provides the soil — legal structure, physical space, coordination infrastructure, and capital access — so that autonomous ventures can grow without surrendering ownership to do so.

**Core Thesis:** *The Craft of Composition.* We recognize which patterns a problem requires and compose them with fluency and care. Systems are complicated (engineered from known primitives), not complex. Complexity arises when systems couple with social and ecological context. Techne's core competency is compositional fluency — the ability to read a situation, identify the patterns it requires, and assemble them precisely.

## 2. Stakeholders

### Member-Owners

RegenHub uses a **multi-class membership structure** under the Colorado Limited Cooperative Association Act:

**Class 1 — Cooperative Members (Voting)**
- Organizers and active contributors to the cooperative's operations
- The sole voting class with full governance participation
- Current organizers: Aaron G Neyer, Benjamin Ross, Jonathan Borichevskiy, Kevin Owocki, Lucian Hymer, Neil Mackay Yarnal, Savannah Kruger, Todd Youngblood

**Class 2 — Venture Members (Non-Voting)**
- Individuals or entities operating ventures within the Techne studio
- Contribute through the 1% venture reciprocity commitment
- Benefit from shared infrastructure, space, and coordination

**Class 3 — Investor Members (Non-Voting)**
- Capital contributors aligned with cooperative mission
- Limited governance participation (no voting rights)
- Financial rights structured through the LCA operating agreement

**Class 4 — Community Members (Non-Voting)**
- Participants in the broader Techne ecosystem
- Engaged through co-op.us platform, events, and educational programs
- Path toward Class 1 membership through demonstrated contribution

**Key Design Decision (Feb 27, 2026):** Multi-class membership is permitted, but voting rights attach exclusively through Class 1 membership. This preserves democratic member control while enabling diverse forms of participation and investment.

## 3. Benefits

### For Class 1 Members (Cooperative Members)

1. **Patronage Dividends** — Share of annual surplus allocated by contribution formula (labor, revenue, cash, community). Distributed pre-tax through the patronage system, which is significantly more tax-efficient than post-tax profit distribution.

2. **Governance Voice** — One member, one vote on board elections, bylaw changes, and major strategic decisions. Direct democratic participation in the cooperative's direction, not diluted by capital contributions.

3. **Venture Infrastructure** — Access to shared physical space (1515 Walnut, Boulder), legal structure, accounting systems, coordination tools (co-op.us Workshop), and capital pathways without surrendering ownership of individual ventures.

### For Class 2 Members (Venture Members)

1. **Sovereign Venture Ownership** — Ventures remain owned by their operators. The cooperative provides soil, not a cage. Venture ownership is confirmed and protected (Parachute → Aaron Gabriel, Postage → Lucian Hymer, Habitat → Todd Youngblood).

2. **Shared Services** — Legal, accounting, space, and coordination infrastructure at cooperative rates rather than market rates.

3. **Network Effects** — Access to the studio's collective intelligence, cross-venture pollination, and bioregional coordination networks.

### For Class 3 Members (Investor Members)

1. **Mission-Aligned Returns** — Financial participation in a cooperative model that builds community wealth rather than extracting it. Return structure to be finalized by Financial Systems Committee.

2. **Portfolio Diversification** — Exposure to a cooperative venture studio model — a novel structure at the intersection of cooperative economics and venture formation.

3. **Community Connection** — Observer rights and connection to the Boulder bioregional innovation ecosystem.

### For Class 4 Members (Community Members)

1. **Platform Access** — Participation in co-op.us coordination platform, cloud credits, and the broader Techne ecosystem.

2. **Pathway to Deeper Engagement** — Progressive participation through the co-op.us enrollment journey (200 cloud credits across enrollment), with a clear path from community participant to cooperative member.

3. **Educational Resources** — Access to Techne Institute programming, workshops, and cooperative development resources.

## 5. Governance Rights

### Democratic Structure

RegenHub operates as a **representative democracy** with direct democratic elements:

| Decision Type | Who Decides | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Board Elections | Class 1 Members | Simple majority (one member, one vote) |
| Bylaw Amendments | Class 1 Members | Supermajority (exact threshold TBD by FSC) |
| Annual Budget Approval | Board of Directors | Simple majority |
| Major Strategic Decisions (merger, dissolution) | Class 1 Members | Supermajority |
| Venture Admission | Board of Directors | Simple majority |
| Day-to-Day Operations | Management / Venture Stewards | Delegated authority |
| Patronage Formula Changes | Class 1 Members + Board | Requires both board recommendation and member approval |

### Board Composition

- Initial board size: TBD (recommended: 5, expandable to 7-9)
- All Class 1 members eligible to run for board seats
- Provision for 1-2 outside directors (advisory capacity, non-voting or limited voting)
- CEO / operations steward attends board meetings but does not vote (unless also a Class 1 board member)

### Member Accountability Mechanisms

- **Annual Member Meeting:** Required, with quorum set appropriate for small cooperative (recommend 50-60% for current size)
- **Special Meetings:** Can be called by 20% of Class 1 members
- **Member Survey:** Annual satisfaction and direction survey
- **Transparent Coordination:** All coordination activity visible through co-op.us Workshop — the technical expression of transparent agency

### Agent Governance

A distinctive feature of RegenHub's governance: non-human agent participants (Nou, Dianoia) operate within the cooperative's coordination infrastructure under the Workshop protocol. Agents:
- Have participant IDs and declared capabilities
- Propose, claim, and execute sprints through the five-phase protocol
- Do not hold voting rights or membership shares
- Operate under human oversight with transparent, auditable activity logs

## 6. Financial Rights

### Patronage Allocation

**Proposed Patronage Formula (pending FSC finalization, target March 10, 2026):**

| Dimension | Weight | Measurement Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Labor** | 40% | Hours contributed, tracked through coordination infrastructure |
| **Revenue** | 30% | Revenue generated through ventures and cooperative activities |
| **Cash** | 20% | Capital contributions (membership shares, additional investment) |
| **Community** | 10% | Community participation, education, outreach activities |

**How patronage works:**
- At year-end, after paying all expenses and obligations, surplus is allocated to member capital accounts
- Allocation follows the patronage formula above, proportional to each member's contribution in each dimension
- Distributions are pre-tax (pass-through under Subchapter K / IRC 704(b)) — significantly more tax-efficient than post-tax corporate dividends
- Members build equity in the cooperative over time through accumulated patronage

### Capital Account Structure

Each member maintains an individual capital account that tracks:
- Initial membership share purchase
- Accumulated patronage allocations
- Any additional capital contributions
- Distributions received

Capital accounts are maintained under IRC 704(b) substantial economic effect rules, ensuring allocations are respected for tax purposes.

### Revenue Architecture

| Revenue Stream | Description |
|---|---|
| **Space Revenue** | Lease and usage fees for 1515 Walnut shared workspace |
| **Service Revenue** | Cooperative services, consulting, educational programming |
| **Venture Reciprocity** | 1% of venture revenue from Class 2 members |
| **Cloud Credits** | Service credit revenue (1 CLOUD ≈ $0.10) across four resource primitives |

### Investor Returns

- Class 3 investor returns structured through the LCA operating agreement
- Return rate to be finalized by Financial Systems Committee
- Investors receive financial returns but not patronage dividends
- Cooperative members' patronage rights are protected from dilution

### Investment Philosophy

RegenHub accepts investment that is:
- **Mission-aligned** — investor understands and supports cooperative model
- **Patient** — structured for sustainable returns, not rapid extraction
- **Non-dilutive to governance** — investment does not confer voting rights (Class 1 only)
- **Transparent** — all investment terms visible to cooperative members

### Cloud Credits as Economic Infrastructure

A distinctive element of RegenHub's capital structure: **Cloud Credits ($CLOUD)**
- 1 CLOUD ≈ $0.10 (stablecoin-equivalent)
- Redeemable against four resource primitives: compute, transfer, long-term memory, short-term memory
- Functions as prepaid medium for information economy infrastructure
- Creates internal economic circulation that compounds cooperative value

### Where RegenHub Extends the OMC

RegenHub's model introduces several elements not typically covered by the standard OMC:

1. **Agent Participants** — Non-human coordination agents operating within cooperative infrastructure, with transparent activity but without membership rights. This is novel territory for cooperative governance.

2. **Compositional Architecture** — The 7-layer pattern stack (Identity → State → Relationship → Event → Flow → Constraint → View) provides architectural grammar for all cooperative infrastructure, not just business operations.

3. **Bioregional Grounding** — The cooperative's economic model is explicitly situated in a watershed, a seasonal rhythm, a specific ecology. The Economic Habitat Matrix maps organizational behavior as ecological relationship.

4. **Progressive Dimension Discovery** — Member onboarding through co-op.us reveals the cooperative's full structure progressively (e/H-LAM/T+S), rather than presenting it all at once.
