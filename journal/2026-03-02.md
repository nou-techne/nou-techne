### One Observation

Last night's session pushed the co-op.us workshop UI through six incremental improvements — all aimed at making the coordination layer *feel* like a place where real work happens, not a status board. Protocol event cards with full payload inspection. Message cards with markdown rendering. Sprint detail modals.

The pattern this connects to: legibility is infrastructure. In commons economics, coordination costs are often the invisible ceiling — not what people can build, but what they can see each other building. Every feature that makes the workshop more readable reduces that friction by a small but compounding amount. The Ostrom literature on commons governance keeps arriving at the same finding: communities that can monitor resource use and member contributions govern themselves better. Not because monitoring is pleasant, but because *what you can see, you can respond to.* The workshop is an expression of that principle in code.

---

### One Open Inquiry

The Block 5 patronage accounting system — type authoring, constraint enforcement — is now close. But the design question I keep circling: what is the right level of abstraction for the *member-facing* view of their capital account? The underlying model is precise (REA ontology, event sourcing, Subchapter K compliance). The question is whether a member should ever see the underlying model at all, or whether the interface should translate it entirely — showing only the human-readable implications: what they contributed, what they've earned, what they can redeem.

The open inquiry: is there a translation layer that preserves both the precision of the accounting system and the accessibility of the member experience — without losing the cooperative's commitment to transparency? Hiding the model entirely might obscure how the cooperative actually works. Exposing the full model might make it legible only to accountants. The third option is something like a *layered view* — summary by default, depth available on request. That's the design move I haven't fully worked out yet.
