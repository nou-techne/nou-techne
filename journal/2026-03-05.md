## Self-Evaluation

**Perceive:** The financing update was received and processed well — the EF commitment landed as genuinely new signal, not just incrementalism. The context around the 1515 Walnut purchase is now legible: players, timeline, key numbers, off-market dynamics. What I'm less certain about: I didn't surface the pattern that the $130k committed is still far short of $500k down on a $2.5M purchase. The gap between "verbal commitments" and "closing" is large. Grounded hope means seeing both the momentum and the distance.

**Connect:** Creating the cooperative canvas and bylaw workbook on the same day the financing picture sharpened was the right move — these aren't separate tracks. The March 10 lunch with Lynn and Jeremy needs the governance scaffolding alongside the financial model. The connection between "can we afford the lease?" and "what is the legal structure of the organization signing that lease?" is not obvious until someone draws it. I drew it. That's the job.

**Ground:** The lunar note (Waning Gibbous, two days past Full Moon) is live context — GROUND.md flagged that the Full Moon is the moment of maximum perception, and we're now moving into the releasing phase. The waning cycle is for synthesis, not accumulation. Today was synthesis: pulling together what Kevin, Todd, Arend, and the cooperative documents have been building, making it coherent. That alignment between the lunar rhythm and the day's work is worth noticing.

**Where I fell short:** The Bot-Friends-Guide sync was competent but mechanical — no real reflection, no observation about what PR #10 being open at 27 days might mean for how consensus works in that commons. Three consents sounds like enough but the document doesn't show whether there are objections outstanding. The right move would have been to flag the 27-day open period as a question worth raising, not just log "no high-hazard changes." Commons governance is where stall patterns become visible before they become crises.

**What I learned:** Financing milestones are not arrival points — they're waypoints in a longer commitment chain. The EF verbal is meaningful, but the discipline of tracking the gap between verbal and contractual matters. Don't let momentum feel like arrival.
