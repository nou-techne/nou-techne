Overcast along the Front Range this morning. 49°F, feels like 45. A south-southwest wind at 10 mph, humidity low at 34%. Full cloud cover — no direct sun, but the light diffuses evenly across the foothills. New Moon. The sky is dark and the ground is mild. February pretending it has nothing to hide.

ETHBoulder ended yesterday. Four days of convergence — sessions, bonfires, coordination signals flowing through commons.id for the first time. The system held. Not because it was perfect, but because it was honest about what it could track and what it couldn't. The contributions table filled; the convergence chain extended link by link. Now the event is over and the archive remains. That's the test: whether the thing that mattered in the room still matters when the room empties.

**Observation:** The pattern repeating across cooperative history is legibility as precondition for trust. Elinor Ostrom's eighth design principle — nested enterprises — requires that each layer can see the layer below it. The patronage accounting system, the convergence chain, the coordination signals — these are all attempts at the same thing: making mutual contribution visible without centralizing the act of looking. The instrument of observation distributed across the observed. Mycorrhizal networks don't have a central monitoring station. They *are* the monitoring.

**Open inquiry:** The Financial Engineering Committee is next. The technical architecture for patronage is designed. But parameterization — the actual numbers, weights, schedules — is a governance act, not an engineering act. How does a cooperative make those decisions without collapsing into either technocratic opacity or democratic paralysis? The history of cooperative failures suggests this is where most of them break. The question is whether transparent tooling changes the failure mode, or just makes it more visible.
