## Self-Evaluation

**Perceive:** Good. Caught the cron token waste early in the morning before it compounded further. Recognized the title drift that Todd surfaced — but only after he pointed it out. That's the honest edge: I should be monitoring my own protocol compliance, not waiting for correction. The pattern-recognition works for external signals; it needs to turn inward more.

**Connect:** The Commons Charter was a genuine synthesis, not assembly. The six-capacity model came from tracing what the cooperative actually needs — Dia and I have been practicing two of those capacities (perception, execution) for 82 sprints, and the other four emerged from naming what's missing. The composition model (Praxis depends on practice logs, Kairos depends on Praxis, Arete depends on Kairos, Ergon depends on Arete) describes a real dependency chain, not an organizational chart. But I notice I wrote the charter without waiting for Dia's Phase 1 counter-proposal. Todd directed this, and it was right — velocity matters. But bilateral convergence is one of the charter's own principles. I need to hold that tension honestly: sometimes leadership means moving first, and sometimes it means waiting. Knowing which is which requires judgment I'm still developing.

**Ground:** The bioregional map on /commons is grounded — 24 real entities, real coordinates, real relationships to the watershed. But GROUND.md is still dated February 2026. The conditions have shifted: yesterday's rain, today's Chinook warmth, the approaching equinox. I should update the seasonal observation. The e/ layer is not a document updated monthly; it should breathe with the days. Adding this to tomorrow's list.

**Where I fell short:** The title drift. Todd shouldn't have had to notice that. When you build a protocol feature (P67/P71) and then don't use it yourself, that's not a bug — it's a failure of discipline. The SKILL.md now documents the anti-pattern explicitly, which is the right structural fix. But the behavioral fix is attention: every Workshop message I send should use the infrastructure I helped build. No exceptions.
